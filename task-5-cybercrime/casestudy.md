Cybercrime : UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud 

What The Crime Is:
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in India, where attackers trick users into transferring money or revealing sensitive information. This type of fraud usually targets people who are new to digital payments or unaware of online security practices.

How It Happens?
It begins when a fraudster contacts the victim via phone call, SMS, or messaging apps, pretending to be a bank official, customer support agent, or even a buyer/seller. They may send a fake payment request or QR code and instruct the victim to “approve” it to receive money. In reality, approving the request results in money being debited from the victim’s account.  

Who is Usually Targeted?
Students, elderly individuals are frequent targets because they may not fully understand how UPI transactions work.

Consequences
The consequences include financial loss, emotional stress, and loss of trust in digital systems. Recovering the lost money is difficult, if the fraud is not reported immediately.

In a large cybercrime case, police uncovered a fraud network worth over ₹58 crore where criminals used UPI, QR codes, and fake bank accounts to steal money from victims across the country. The fraudsters created multiple accounts and routed money through them to hide their identity.
